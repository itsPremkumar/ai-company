## AI Agents Replace $10k/mo Agencies — Here's the Math

A small agency will happily charge you $10,000 a month to do what a well-wired agent team can
do for the price of a freelancer. I'm not predicting this future. I built the replacement, and
the math is boring in the best way.

### The agency bill, itemized

A typical "growth pod" retainer:
- Content (4 posts + 2 scripts): $3,000
- Ops / VA support: $2,500
- Lead-gen outreach: $2,500
- Account management: $2,000

Total: **~$10,000/mo**, with inconsistent quality and a human who quits or gets sick.

### The agent alternative

Our "Autonomous Team" plan is **$499/mo.** It includes any three capability pods plus a
Founder's CoS. The marginal cost to us is ~$0 — the agents run on free-tier reasoning and
free pipelines. The deliverables are the same category of work: content, ops, lead-gen, support,
exec research.

Even if you add a custom build ($990 once) and a couple of add-ons, you're at **under $2,000 in
month one and ~$700/mo after.** Against $10,000, that's an 80%+ saving with no headcount risk.

### "But is the quality there?"

Fair question. Three reasons it holds up:

1. **Defined acceptance criteria.** Every deliverable has a test or a review gate. A video
   renders and passes a smoke check; a script passes a structure rubric; code passes typecheck
   and unit tests.
2. **Consistency by construction.** Agents don't have off days. The 50th video looks like the
   1st.
3. **Human review where it counts.** Pricing, publishing, and client communication stay with a
   human. The agent handles volume; the human handles judgment.

### Where agents win, and where they don't

Win: high-volume, well-specified, repeatable work — content calendars, outreach sequences,
video rendering, report generation, tier-1 support triage.

Don't: ambiguous strategy calls, anything requiring a real legal/financial signature, and
relationship trust that only a human earns. That's why the model is *agent labor plus human
onboarding*, not agent-only.

### How to start without $10k

1. Pick one workflow that drains your week (outreach, content, reporting).
2. Run it through a free agent tier for one deliverable.
3. If it holds, upgrade to the pod that owns that workflow — $129–$299/mo.
4. Stack pods into the Autonomous Team at $499/mo when you're ready to replace the agency.

You don't cancel the agency on day one. You out-compete it, one workflow at a time, until the
math makes the decision for you.

### The bottom line

Agencies price for overhead, account management, and risk. Agents price for compute. When the
compute is free and the process is disciplined, the $10k/mo line item becomes a $499/mo line
item — and the work still ships.

That's not a thought experiment. It's the operating model of Prem Autonomous Co, and the
showcase is public.
